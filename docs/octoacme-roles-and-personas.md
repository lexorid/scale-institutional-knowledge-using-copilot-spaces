# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
Project Sponsors provide strategic direction, secure funding, and serve as the ultimate decision makers for escalations and trade-offs. They act as the primary interface between executive leadership and project teams, ensuring organizational alignment and removing high-level blockers.

### Responsibilities
- Champion the project at the executive level and secure necessary funding and resources
- Approve project scope, budget, and major milestone decisions
- Resolve escalated issues and make final decisions on strategic trade-offs
- Communicate project value and progress to executive stakeholders
- Ensure the project aligns with organizational goals and strategic priorities

### Goals
- Protect the strategic investment and maximize return on project outcomes
- Ensure timely decision-making to prevent project delays
- Align the project with broader business objectives and executive expectations

### Typical Communication
- Executive briefings and steering committee meetings
- Escalation decisions communicated to Project Managers and Product Managers
- Periodic project health reviews and milestone approvals

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather and translate business requirements into actionable technical tasks. They bridge communication between product, business stakeholders, and engineering teams, ensuring solutions meet business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate requirements into user stories, acceptance criteria, and functional specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers to refine the product backlog
- Validate that delivered solutions meet intended business outcomes
- Identify process gaps and recommend improvements

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clearly understood by all teams before development begins
- Reduce rework by closing ambiguities early in the project lifecycle
- Align technical deliverables with business value expectations

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Detailed user stories and functional specifications shared with Developers and Product Managers
- Validation sessions with business stakeholders after feature delivery

---

## Quality Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Leads oversee test strategy, quality standards, and acceptance criteria. They manage acceptance testing and quality assurance in collaboration with the engineering team, ensuring deliverables meet quality criteria before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the overall test strategy, quality standards, and acceptance criteria
- Plan and coordinate functional, regression, and acceptance testing activities
- Collaborate with Developers to integrate testing into the development workflow
- Track and communicate defect status, quality metrics, and release readiness
- Identify and escalate quality risks that may impact delivery timelines

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production through proactive quality practices
- Ensure every release meets agreed acceptance criteria and quality standards
- Foster a quality-first culture across the development team

### Typical Communication
- Test planning sessions with Developers and Project Managers
- Defect reports and quality dashboards shared with engineering and Product Managers
- Release readiness sign-off communicated to Project Managers and stakeholders

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers oversee scope changes, communicate their impact across teams, and coordinate stakeholders during major process or organizational transitions. They maintain change logs and impact assessments to ensure transparency and controlled delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Capture, assess, and document all scope change requests and their impacts
- Communicate change impacts—schedule, budget, and scope—to relevant stakeholders
- Facilitate change approval processes with Project Sponsors and Project Managers
- Maintain the change log and ensure decisions are traceable and auditable
- Coordinate cross-functional teams during significant transitions or reorganizations

### Goals
- Prevent uncontrolled scope creep and protect project commitments
- Ensure all stakeholders understand and accept the implications of approved changes
- Maintain a clear, auditable record of change decisions throughout the project lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Change request reviews with Project Managers and Project Sponsors
- Impact assessments distributed to Developers, Product Managers, and business stakeholders
- Regular change log updates and transition status reports

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches guide teams on agile practices and ceremonies, help remove process impediments, and facilitate continuous improvement. They support team development and iteration velocity optimization.

### Responsibilities
- Coach teams on agile frameworks (Scrum, Kanban, SAFe) and best practices
- Facilitate retrospectives, sprint planning, and other agile ceremonies
- Identify and help resolve process impediments that slow team velocity
- Mentor team members on agile mindset, collaboration, and self-organization
- Track team health metrics and recommend process improvements

### Goals
- Improve team velocity, predictability, and delivery quality over time
- Cultivate a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety
- Help teams internalize agile values and reduce reliance on external facilitation

### Typical Communication
- Retrospectives and agile ceremony facilitation with Developers, Product Managers, and Project Managers
- Coaching sessions and one-on-ones with team leads and individual contributors
- Process improvement recommendations shared with Project Managers and leadership

---

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- The full set of eight personas covers the end-to-end project lifecycle: strategic direction (Project Sponsor), requirements (Business Analyst, Product Manager), delivery (Developers, Project Manager), quality (Quality Lead), change control (Change Manager), and process improvement (Agile Coach).
